# AMAZE Materials and Construction Catalog

This catalog answers: what can we build with, what does it look like, who can
help make it, how durable is it, and when must it upgrade from craft prototype
to public-use hardware.

Use this with `components/INVENTORY.md`, `components/KITS.md`, and each room
`BUILD.md`.

## Material card fields

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Material ID | Stable ID for room BOMs and construction plans. |
| Look/feel | What the material visually or tactically suggests. |
| Best use | What it is good for in trailer escape-room builds. |
| Avoid for | Unsafe, fragile, messy, or misleading uses. |
| Maker fit | Child helper, teen helper, adult supervised, adult only. |
| Durability | Prototype, low, medium, high, consumable. |
| Upgrade path | What replaces it for public operation. |

## Maker fit levels

| Level | Meaning | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Child helper | Safe with close adult supervision; no blades, heat, fumes, power tools, or small magnet handling. | coloring labels, sorting tokens, tearing paper, painting broad surfaces |
| Teen helper | Can handle more precise craft with supervision and safety briefing. | cutting foamcore with safe tools, assembling tokens, painting details, labeling reset photos |
| Adult supervised | Adult controls sharp/hot/powered steps; helper can assist. | hot glue, utility knife scoring, drilling pilot holes, wiring low-voltage props |
| Adult only | Tool, chemical, electrical, load, pinch, fire, or venue safety risk. | saws, mains power, structural mounting, soldering near public-use electronics |

## What children or teens can safely help make

| Good helper task | Why it helps | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| Paint broad scenic color blocks | fast theme identity and ownership | no required clue depends on brush precision |
| Color-code tokens and silhouettes | reinforces reset and category homes | adult verifies final clue colors/symbols |
| Tear/apply paper mache layers | great texture for coral/stone/plaster | adult seals; keep away from mechanisms |
| Assemble laminated clue packets | builds duplicate sets and backups | adult controls cutting/laminating heat |
| Sort spare kits and reset pouches | teaches operations and catches missing parts | adult checks final count |
| Test wrong-token fits gently | excellent chaos/reliability evidence | no force; adult observes and logs |

## Do not use in trailer public builds without review

| Material/use | Concern | Safer alternative |
|---|---|---|
| loose glitter/confetti | impossible reset, mechanism contamination, slip/cleanup | glitter cardstock, foil vinyl, holographic laminate |
| unsealed paper mache | dust, moisture, breakage | sealed hardcoat, carved foam, plastic scenic shell |
| glass jars/mirrors | breakage/sharp edges/transport | acrylic/polycarbonate |
| tiny beads/magnets | choking/loss/reset risk | oversized tokens, enclosed magnets |
| hot glue as structural joint | heat/impact failure | screws, brackets, mechanical fastening |
| wet paint during operations | mess/fumes/reset delay | pre-sealed pieces, dry printed finish |
| real food/liquid | allergen, pests, slip, reset | fake food tokens, dry cards, sealed props |

## Prototype-to-production rule

Prototype materials are allowed to be charming and handmade. Public-use materials
must be safe, cleanable, resettable, replaceable, and documented.

Every room should name:

1. which material proves the aha cheaply;
2. which material becomes the public-use version;
3. what children/teens can help make safely;
4. what adult-only step protects safety and durability;
5. what material choice would force redesign.
