# Astral Archive Overnight Brief

## Status

- Lifecycle state: Seed
- Current version: v0.seed
- Last evidence source: design draft only
- Next gate: lint/check, 90-minute timing optimization, tabletop simulation, then act-by-act cardboard bench

## Concept

- Title: Astral Archive Overnight
- Trailer envelope: premium mobile planning envelope; assumes an expanded trailer or linked trailer pair only if the booked build declares it.
- Team size: 3-6 players
- Target session time: 90 minutes of play
- Ops slot: 120-minute premium booking with 25-minute reset and 5-minute turnover buffer
- Reset target: 25 minutes
- Theme promise: players are night archivists restoring a traveling star archive before dawn erases its mechanical sky map.
- Signature moment: three act relics physically dock into a large orrery vault, rotating a star field to reveal the final dawn coordinate.

## Audience

- Primary audience: premium escape-room fans, corporate teams, enthusiasts, and groups who want a longer story arc.
- Accessibility assumptions: seating/lean moments every act, no crawling, no darkness-only or audio-only clue, no high reach, and no required memory-only clue from earlier acts.
- Fear/intensity level: wonder, time pressure, and mystery; no horror or simulated entrapment.
- Reading/listening load: medium-high but chunked by acts; all critical story facts become physical state cards.

## Constraints

- Power: optional low-voltage star-field lighting only; every required beat has manual proof or printed fallback.
- Weight/mounting: orrery vault and archive walls must travel as modular panels; no heavy glass or unsecured ceiling props.
- Staff visibility: operator must see act state boards, comfort-pause clock, proof trays, and final vault.
- Weather/venue: 90-minute premium slot needs temperature monitoring, restroom plan, and safe mid-session interruption script.
- Legal/code assumptions to verify: trailer occupancy, emergency exit signage, low-voltage wiring, event accessibility, staffing ratio, and comfort-break policy.

## Invariants check

- Egress visible in floorplan: yes; egress lane and act-pause path stay clear.
- Staff override declared: yes; each act relic and final orrery has manual release.
- Accessibility assumptions named: yes; seating, work-light, non-audio, non-memory, and role rotation are required.
- Physical signature moment declared: yes; act relics dock into final orrery vault.
- Reset target declared: yes; 25 minutes with act bins, photos, and pouch counts.
- Safety unknowns named: fatigue, heat/cold, restroom interruption, final vault crowding, wheel pinch, and reset complexity.

## Success criteria

- Players should say: "It felt like three rooms became one story, and we never lost the thread."
- Operators should say: "I can pause, accelerate, reset, and recover any act without breaking safety or fiction."
- Builder should say: "The long session is modular: act bins, proof relics, spares, and bypasses are obvious."
