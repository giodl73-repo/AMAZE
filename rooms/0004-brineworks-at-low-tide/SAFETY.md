# The Brineworks at Low Tide - Safety

## Egress

- Normal exit: main trailer door remains visible and unobstructed.
- Emergency exit: must be visible or explicitly identified in briefing once trailer model is known.
- Staff override: operator can stop game, turn up lights, release final state, and direct exit.
- Player-visible reassurance: intro states players are never locked in and can leave any time.

## Hazards

| Hazard | Risk | Mitigation | Open question |
|---|---|---|---|
| fake food/ingredient tiles | trip, pocketing, allergy confusion | no real food, oversized washable props, count cards | final material |
| pipe board | pinch, high reach, sharp edges | rounded chunky parts, waist-height mounting | board hardware |
| clam register | pinch/force | hinge stops, no-force script, rounded shell | hinge design |
| pressure griddle | heat/steam assumption, force, pinch | no heat/steam, dry effect only, robust sockets | final effect |
| bubble effects | slip, moisture, electrical risk | dry visual bubbles/LEDs/cards only for required path | optional real bubbles likely rejected |
| low light | unreadable labels, trip risk | work-light/high-contrast mode for all required clues | final lighting level |
| audio | bell clue exclusion | visual clam closure carries required solve | final sound level |

## Accessibility

- Participation without crawling/climbing: required by design.
- No wet surfaces: required by design.
- Low-light alternative: high-contrast labels and work-light readable stations.
- Audio alternative: bell solve is visual/tactile; sounds are flavor.
- Seated/limited-mobility path: checker roles at pipe board, order rail, and griddle.

## Operator controls

- Monitoring: camera/audio on door, pressure griddle, proof rail, pipe board, order rail, and register.
- Emergency stop: turn up lights, stop sound, disable powered props, direct exit.
- Communication: operator can speak in fiction as the shift lead or reef steward.
- Extraction script: "The lunch rush is paused. Please step to the visible exit now."
