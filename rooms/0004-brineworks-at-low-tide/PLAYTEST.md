## Surprise log

| Surprise | Evidence | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| Undersea comedy can become prop soup quickly. | Ingredient bins and bell tokens are attractive to chaotic teams. | Keep every loose object oversized, counted, and category-bounded. |
| Bell props are dangerous as clues if audio-only. | Accessibility guardrail. | Required bell solve is visual/tactile; sound is flavor. |
| Funny props still need hard mechanics. | P4 moved from "bell closes clam" to a pearl-fit cup test. | Comedy works better when the physical test is obvious and fair. |
| The final griddle is the room's promise. | All proofs converge there. | Prototype sockets before scenic finish. |
| A device can be a crowd pleaser and still be a promotion blocker. | P4/P5 are memorable but invite force/guessing. | Track crowd profile and frustration trigger separately from fun. |
