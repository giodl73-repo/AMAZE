# Clockwork Greenhouse Brief

## Status

- Lifecycle state: Seed
- Current version: v0.seed
- Last evidence source: design draft only
- Next gate: lint/check, timing optimization, tabletop simulation, then cardboard bench

## Concept

- Title: Clockwork Greenhouse
- Trailer envelope: planning envelope is a 7 ft x 16 ft interior mobile trailer; do not assume a real trailer model yet.
- Team size: 2-5 players
- Target session time: 45 minutes
- Ops slot: staffed-hour mobile event slot
- Reset target: 10 minutes
- Theme promise: players enter a traveling botanical automaton where a rare clockwork orchid will bloom only if light, water, soil, and pollination are physically balanced.
- Signature moment: the final flower dial opens when four large botanical proof tokens are seated around a mechanical bloom wheel.

## Audience

- Primary audience: families, date-night groups, corporate teams, puzzle fans who enjoy tactile/cozy themes.
- Accessibility assumptions: no crawling, climbing, darkness-only clues, scent-only clues, audio-only clues, or high reach required.
- Fear/intensity level: calm urgency, whimsical machine pressure, no horror.
- Reading/listening load: low-medium; plant labels are short and mechanism affordances carry the solve.

## Constraints

- Power: optional low-voltage flower glow only; all puzzle beats work passively.
- Weight/mounting: seed trays, valve board, prism panel, and bloom wheel must travel as flat modules.
- Staff visibility: operator sees the central bench, proof rail, and final bloom wheel.
- Weather/venue: greenhouse fiction must not add real water spills, loose soil, allergens, or scent dependency.
- Legal/code assumptions to verify: trailer occupancy, egress signage, battery/LED wiring, non-slip flooring, event accessibility requirements.

## Invariants check

- Egress visible in floorplan: yes; clear aisle and exit are part of the briefing.
- Staff override declared: yes; operator can release final bloom token with a manual card.
- Accessibility assumptions named: yes; seated, low-light, no-scent, no-audio paths are required.
- Physical signature moment declared: yes; proof tokens seat around the bloom wheel.
- Reset target declared: yes; 10 minutes with tray counts and reset photos.
- Safety unknowns named: acrylic/prism glare, finger pinch at bloom wheel, prop spill/trip risk, and real trailer aisle width.

## Success criteria

- Players should say: "The greenhouse bloomed because we tuned every system."
- Operators should say: "Every plant station is countable, visible, and recoverable."
- Builder should say: "The flower wheel, tokens, and station trays are durable enough for mobile resets."
