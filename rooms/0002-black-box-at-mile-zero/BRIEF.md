## Story spine

At 2:17 a.m., a roadside recorder was recovered from a mile marker that does not
exist. Its casing is scratched with dust, seed pods, and road grit from no known
county. The incident report has no victim, no vehicle, and no crash site. It only
has fragments: distance tags, witness-window states, a skipping recording, and a
sealed compartment that no one has opened safely.

The players are brought in as roadside investigators because the recorder has
started broadcasting again. Their job is not to catch a villain. Their job is to
reconstruct a road that should not be possible, prove where Mile Zero is, and
open the box without damaging the evidence.

The final emotional beat should feel like relief and awe: the black box was not
warning them away from the road; it was preserving directions to it.

## Audience

- Primary audience: mixed public groups, families with teens, puzzle-curious adults.
- Accessibility assumptions: no crawling, climbing, or force required; central object must be reachable from seated height or mirrored by a companion action.
- Fear/intensity level: eerie mystery and wonder, no actor jump scares, no gore.
- Reading/listening load: moderate; audio must have visual or tactile redundancy.

## Constraints

- Power: low-voltage props only until build review.
- Weight/mounting: central object must be secured for transport and player force.
- Staff visibility: operator must see central object, door, and all required player zones.
- Weather/venue: exterior queue and intro should not depend on weather.
- Legal/code assumptions to verify: egress, fire extinguisher placement, low-voltage wiring, venue parking/leveling.
