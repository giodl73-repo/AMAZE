# The Black Box at Mile Zero - Safety

## Egress

- Normal exit: main trailer door remains visible and unobstructed.
- Emergency exit: must be visible or explicitly identified in briefing once trailer model is known.
- Staff override: operator can stop game, turn up lights, release final state, and direct exit.
- Player-visible reassurance: intro states players are never actually locked in and can leave any time.

## Hazards

| Hazard | Risk | Mitigation | Open question |
|---|---|---|---|
| central object weight | shift during transport or player force | bolt/strap to structure, transport lock | final object mass and mounting points |
| latch/hinge pinch | finger pinch during final opening | guarded hinges, slow opening, one-handle design | latch hardware selection |
| forced interaction | prop breakage or player injury | no-force script, mechanical stops, operator intervention | how much resistance feels satisfying |
| low light | tripping or unreadable evidence | work-light level at all required clues | final lighting tone |
| audio dependence | exclusion or missed clue | transcript backup with equivalent info | transcript fiction and placement |
| low-voltage cabinet | electrical/fire concern | low-voltage only, fused circuit, no real mains | exact power architecture |

## Accessibility

- Participation without crawling/climbing: required by design.
- Low-light alternative: required clue marks must be readable under work light.
- Audio alternative: transcript backup must solve P3 without needing hearing.
- Seated/limited-mobility path: central object and evidence rail should be reachable or support companion handling with seated caller/checker role.

## Operator controls

- Monitoring: camera/audio on door, central object, evidence rail, and utility cabinet.
- Emergency stop: turn up lights, disable powered props, stop audio, and direct exit.
- Communication: operator can speak in fiction as dispatch or evidence control.
- Extraction script: "The case is paused. Please step to the visible exit now."
