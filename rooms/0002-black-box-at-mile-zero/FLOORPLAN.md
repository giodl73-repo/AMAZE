## Flow

| Moment | Player movement | Gathering point | Crowding risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Intro | Team enters and sees the box before touching it. | Entry strip | Door bottleneck | Keep first instruction away from threshold. |
| Evidence sweep | Players split between box and wall rail. | Central aisle | Two players blocking rail | Duplicate clue angles and keep rail waist-high. |
| Utility alignment | One player handles cabinet while others interpret box state. | Cabinet plus box | Cabinet crowding | Require caller/handler roles. |
| Box opening | Everyone returns to central object. | Central table | All hands on box | Single active handle plus clear no-force affordance. |
| Exit | Final reveal points back to door or visible exit key. | Door | Celebration at threshold | Keep egress clear of final prop. |

## Egress and safety paths

| Path | Use | Player-visible? | Staff override | Blockers to prevent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Normal exit | Session end and normal departure. | Yes | Operator can release/announce at any time. | Props, stools, central table swing. |
| Emergency exit | Safety exit. | Yes or explicitly reassured in intro. | Operator can stop game and direct exit. | Black box table, rail pieces on floor. |
| Staff access | Intervention/reset. | Not player route. | Staff can reach central object and utility cabinet. | Locked scenic panels without quick release. |

## Accessibility and reach

| Need | Current path | Open question |
|---|---|---|
| Seated/limited-mobility participation | Central box, evidence rail, and final reveal are waist/table height. | Exact aisle width and turning radius. |
| No crawling/climbing required | All clues placed at reachable height. | Confirm no floor-level hidden clue becomes required. |
| Low-light alternative | Evidence tags and box marks must be readable under work light. | How spooky can lighting be while readable? |
| Audio alternative | Recorder audio duplicated by printed/tactile transcript fragments. | Best in-fiction transcript device. |
| Reach range for required mechanisms | One-handed handles and dials at table height. | Final box dimensions and latch force. |

## Reset paths

| Reset route | Staff action | Verification point | Time risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Central box reset | Relock latches, reset dials, replace internal artifacts. | box state indicator shows seed state | too many hidden compartments |
| Evidence rail reset | Rehang tags/photos in seed order. | colored/stamped backs match rail positions | missing removable evidence |
| Utility cabinet reset | Return switches/lights/manual bypass. | operator checklist light test | power or sensor drift |

## Transport and mounting notes

| Item/zone | Mounting assumption | Travel risk | Secure/stow plan |
|---|---|---|---|
| Black box | Bolted or strapped to table with service access. | heavy object shift, latch damage | transport lock and foam inserts |
| Evidence rail | Fixed rail with removable evidence bagged separately. | rattling, lost tags | numbered reset pouch |
| Utility cabinet | Low-voltage cabinet mounted to wall/framing. | wire fatigue | strain relief and pre-run test |

## Open spatial risks

- Central object may consume too much floor area if it is built as a trunk instead of a table-mounted prop.
- Crowding at the final opening needs simulation with fighting and chaotic teams.
- Exact trailer model may force fewer wall-rail clues or a smaller object footprint.
