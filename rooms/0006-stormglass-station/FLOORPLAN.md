# Stormglass Station Floorplan

## Envelope

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Trailer model/envelope | Planning envelope only: 7 ft x 16 ft interior mobile trailer |
| Interior length | 16 ft planning length |
| Interior width | 7 ft planning width |
| Interior height | 7 ft planning height |
| Door/threshold constraints | One visible entry/exit door; no puzzle may block swing or threshold |
| Power location | Staff end low-voltage battery/adapter shelf |
| Operator location | Staff end sightline behind half-height station rail or camera monitor |
| Maximum team size | 5 players |

## Zone map

| Zone | Dimensions | Purpose | Player capacity | Key props/mechanisms | Staff access |
|---|---|---|---:|---|---|
| Z1 Entry briefing rail | 3 ft x 7 ft | safety, story, role assignment | 5 | station log, visible exit, role cards | direct |
| Z2 Instrument wall | 6 ft x 2 ft wall band | parallel P1-P3 instrument proofs | 3 | barometer dial, wind vane card rack, tide gauge slider | staff reaches from aisle |
| Z3 Stormglass console | 4 ft x 3 ft counter | final proof seating and route reveal | 3 | proof cartridges, sockets, route shutter | rear service hatch |
| Z4 Signal panel | 3 ft x 2 ft wall panel | final beacon call and payoff | 2 | harbor lamp buttons, semaphore strip | direct |
| Z5 Aisle/egress lane | 16 ft x 3 ft clear lane | circulation and emergency exit | 5 moving | floor tape, no loose props | always open |

## Zone-purpose allocation

| Zone/visible element | Gameplay purpose | Safety/ops purpose | Atmosphere purpose | Shared/reused? | Underused or overloaded risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Entry briefing rail | teaches station roles | exit and no-force rule | storm watch desk | shared | could become clutter if role cards are loose |
| Instrument wall | parallel physical data gathering | keeps players spread | working station instruments | shared | risk of three players crowding one dial |
| Stormglass console | proof convergence | operator-visible final state | magical weather glass | shared | final bottleneck if sockets are unclear |
| Signal panel | final communication | staff can release beacon | harbor rescue payoff | yes | could feel like button guessing without proof gate |
| Aisle/egress lane | movement and clue comparison | emergency path | station walkway | no | must not store reset bins here |

## Flow

| Moment | Player movement | Gathering point | Crowding risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Briefing | team enters and sees exit | Z1 | doorway crowd | operator moves team forward before timer |
| Instrument split | players fan to barometer, wind, tide | Z2 | one-dial pileup | role cards assign dial reader, vane handler, tide checker |
| Proof seating | team brings cartridges to console | Z3 | final counter crowd | console has three visible sides and caller/checker/handler ritual |
| Beacon call | one player sets signal while team verifies | Z4 | low | large labels and operator sightline |
| Exit/reset | team leaves; staff works clockwise | Z5 then all zones | reset bins in aisle | reset cart stays outside trailer |

## Egress and safety paths

| Path | Use | Player-visible? | Staff override | Blockers to prevent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Normal exit | session end and routine exit | yes | operator opens/holds door | no prop bins, no final console queue at door |
| Emergency exit | immediate safety exit | yes | stop script plus lights up | loose cartridges, floor mats, or seated chair in lane |
| Staff access | intervention/reset | partially | service hatch and aisle entry | players leaning over console hatch |

## Operator sightlines

| Zone/beat | Visible to operator? | Monitoring method | Blind spot | Compensation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Z1 briefing | yes | direct sightline | none | spoken safety check |
| P1 barometer | yes | wall view | hands may cover dial | dial has large pointer and duplicate operator mark |
| P2 wind vane | yes | direct/card color | card rack backs | operator asks for vane word if stuck |
| P3 tide gauge | partial | top-down mirror or camera | low slider track | tactile click and duplicate mark |
| P5 console | yes | proof sockets face operator | hands over cartridges | caller/checker protocol |

## Accessibility and reach

| Need | Current path | Open question |
|---|---|---|
| Seated/limited-mobility participation | all required controls between 30 and 48 inches; movable role card lets seated player call/check | confirm real trailer aisle and turn radius |
| No crawling/climbing required | all clues on wall/counter panels | none |
| Low-light alternative | high-contrast labels and work-light mode | test glare on acrylic stormglass |
| Audio alternative | storm sounds are flavor only; all signal data printed/tactile | none |
| Reach range for required mechanisms | cartridges and sliders mounted at counter height | verify with prototype wall mock |

## Reset paths

| Reset route | Staff action | Verification point | Time risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clockwise wall reset | return barometer, vane cards, tide slider | photo match on instrument wall | card rack order |
| Console reset | remove cartridges, close route shutter, check sockets | green/red operator marks hidden | socket jam |
| Signal reset | reset lamp buttons and semaphore strip | all buttons out, strip home | button cap loss |
| Final sweep | count cartridges/cards and clear aisle | pouch inventory card | missing small cartridge |

## Transport and mounting notes

| Item/zone | Mounting assumption | Travel risk | Secure/stow plan |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instrument wall | lightweight framed panel on cleats | dial pointer bend | travel lock and foam spacer |
| Stormglass console | bolted counter module | acrylic scratch | removable acrylic face in sleeve |
| Proof cartridges | small printed/acrylic blocks | loss | numbered pouch plus duplicate set |
| Signal panel | flat wall board | button cap loosen | cap check and spare buttons |

## Open spatial risks

- Console crowding may hide proof state from operator.
- A real trailer narrower than 7 ft may need fewer simultaneous wall stations.
- Acrylic glare may make stormglass route hard for low-vision players.
