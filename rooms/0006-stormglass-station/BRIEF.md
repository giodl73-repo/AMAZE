# Stormglass Station Brief

## Status

- Lifecycle state: Seed
- Current version: v0.seed
- Last evidence source: design draft only
- Next gate: lint, check, timing optimization, then tabletop simulation

## Concept

- Title: Stormglass Station
- Trailer envelope: planning envelope is a 7 ft x 16 ft interior mobile trailer; adjust dimensions if a real trailer differs.
- Team size: 2-5 players
- Target session time: 45 minutes
- Ops slot: staffed-hour mobile event slot
- Reset target: 10 minutes
- Theme promise: players are relief operators in a coastal weather station that must align physical instruments before a fictional storm closes the harbor.
- Signature moment: a stormglass console lights a safe-harbor route only after barometer, wind, tide, and signal proofs are physically seated.

## Audience

- Primary audience: families, corporate groups, puzzle-curious public-event teams, and escape-room fans who like tactile science props.
- Accessibility assumptions: no crawling, climbing, darkness-only clues, audio-only clues, or high reach required; all required information has visual and tactile backup.
- Fear/intensity level: weather pressure and urgency, no horror, no confinement threat.
- Reading/listening load: medium; labels are short and instruments carry the solve.

## Constraints

- Power: low-voltage LED only in the first build; all puzzle state has manual fallback.
- Weight/mounting: wall boards and console must travel flat or latch to trailer ribs; heavy glass is simulated with acrylic.
- Staff visibility: operator sees the central console, proof trays, and final signal panel from the staff end.
- Weather/venue: room fiction is stormy but the real trailer must remain dry, ventilated, and egress-visible.
- Legal/code assumptions to verify: fire extinguisher placement, exit signage, battery/LED wiring, ADA event requirements, local trailer occupancy guidance.

## Invariants check

- Egress visible in floorplan: yes; entry/exit door and staff override remain visible.
- Staff override declared: yes; final beacon can be released by operator script.
- Accessibility assumptions named: yes; seated participation and non-audio/non-dark alternatives are required.
- Physical signature moment declared: yes; proof cartridges physically seat into stormglass console.
- Reset target declared: yes; 10 minutes with tray photos and pouch checks.
- Safety unknowns named: electrical routing, acrylic edge finish, crowding at console, and event-code occupancy.

## Success criteria

- Players should say: "We made the weather instruments agree, and the station answered us."
- Operators should say: "I can see every proof state and recover any jam without breaking fiction."
- Builder should say: "Every critical part has a spare, a diagram, a reset photo, and a manual bypass."
