## Concept

- Title: Manor Lite: The Mirrorline Case
- Trailer envelope: Airstream-size or larger; exact model not assumed.
- Team size: 2-5.

- Ops slot: 60 minutes.

- Theme promise: A whole spooky manor has been compressed into a trailer-sized evidence parlor, and every "room" is visible as a physical station.
- Signature moment: The team seats three proof tokens into a mirrorline verdict board, aligns three accusation cards, and the operator releases the house echo when the public proof state is visible.

## Story spine

Blackwood House used to have twelve rooms. Tonight it has five, all somehow
inside this trailer. A storm is painted on every window. The butler is missing,
the guest bell rings with no hand on it, and the manor keeps changing which room
it claims the incident happened in.

The players are invited as late guests and deputized by the house ledger: identify
the suspect, object, and location before the manor folds one more time. The room
should feel less like filling out a logic worksheet and more like physically
interrogating a tiny haunted house.

## Audience

- Primary audience: mixed public groups, families with teens, puzzle-curious adults, social groups.
- Accessibility assumptions: no crawling, climbing, darkness-only clues, or audio-only solves; deduction evidence must be visible from seated or companion roles.
- Fear/intensity level: spooky, theatrical, no gore, no actor jump scares.
- Reading/listening load: moderate; evidence should be physical and visual, not just paragraphs.

## Constraints

- Power: low-voltage lighting/audio only until build review.
- Weight/mounting: accusation cabinet and room stations must be secured for transport.
- Staff visibility: operator must see final accusation state, door/egress, and high-touch props; lite version favors operator-visible proof over hidden cabinet locks.
- Weather/venue: intro and queue should work without outdoor staging.
- Legal/code assumptions to verify: egress, fire extinguisher placement, low-voltage wiring, aisle clearance, prop flame avoidance.

## Success criteria

- Players should say: "We solved a tiny haunted manor, not a worksheet."
- Players should also remember one eerie physical moment: a portrait, object, room, and mirrorline board all seemed to answer the same accusation.
- Operators should say: "I can see the accusation state and correct a slow team without spoiling the whole deduction."
- Builder should say: "Each room station is modular, mounted, resettable, and replaceable."
