## Spatial beat route

Use a clockwise or counterclockwise evidence loop once a trailer envelope is chosen:

| Order | Location | Active roles | Spatial requirement | Why it matters |
|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Entry vestibule / ledger | reader, caller | team stands clear of threshold | establishes categories without blocking exit |
| 2A | Suspect gallery | token tester, checker | two-person shoulder room at portrait backs | makes P2 physical and shared |
| 2B | Object sideboard | prop handler, checker | shallow tray at waist height, no floor props | avoids search soup and drop hazards |
| 2C | Room stations | overlay handler, low-light checker | work-light surface with seated sightline | makes P4 accessible and non-glare dependent |
| 3 | Proof tray / evidence rail | caller | visible but not central enough to block aisle | makes progress public and reduces pocketed tokens |
| 4 | Mirrorline cabinet | caller, checker, handler | three adjacent role positions at cabinet face | prevents one-player final domination |

## Flow

| Moment | Player movement | Gathering point | Crowding risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Intro | Team enters as late guests. | Entry vestibule | door bottleneck | first clue away from threshold |
| Evidence split | Players divide between gallery, sideboard, stations. | central aisle and station fronts | too many at cabinet | cabinet covers stay closed until proof; proof tray holds progress |
| Proof check | Team deposits earned crest, seal, and tile in public rail. | proof tray / evidence rail | loose tokens in pockets or aisle | labeled token homes outside walking path |
| Deduction converge | Team brings proof tokens from rail to accusation cabinet. | mirrorline cabinet | one player monopolizes covers/sliders | require caller/checker/handler roles, spaced cover positions, and visible token seating |
| Reveal/exit | Manor aligns and releases exit token. | cabinet then door | celebration blocks egress | keep final token away from door swing |

## Open spatial risks

- Deduction stations may create crowding if every clue demands the same wall.
- Mirrorline cabinet must not block egress or invite unsafe leaning.
- Reflections should be theatrical but not create glare, breakage, or readability problems.
- Proof tray must not become a tempting "dump all props here" surface.
- Three-person cabinet role lane needs real trailer dimensions before promotion.

## Tape-out checklist

Before scenic build, tape out the room at the smallest likely trailer envelope.
If exact trailer dimensions are unknown, use the conservative footprint and mark
unknowns visibly.

| Check | Pass condition | If failed |
|---|---|---|
| Door and exit path | exit remains visible and walkable during P5 celebration | move proof rail/cabinet away from threshold |
| Evidence split | two players can work P2 while two work P3/P4 without shoulder blocking | stagger stations or reduce active surfaces |
| Proof rail | token homes are visible but not in the walking lane | mount rail shallower/higher or move beside cabinet |
| Cabinet role lane | caller, checker, and handler can stand/sit at cabinet without reaching across each other | widen cabinet face or move sliders apart |
| Seated sightline | seated checker can read P2/P4/P5 state from assigned positions | lower station surfaces or add companion-readable duplicates |
| Operator sightline | camera can see proof rail, cabinet covers, and door | move camera or rotate cabinet face |
| Reset route | one operator can reach every token home without moving large scenery | add rear access or simplify station mounting |
